Celebrating the achievements and contributions of women in the fight against climate change.

Women Leading the Way

Explore the stories and achievements of women making significant contributions to climate action.

Dr. Jane Goodall

A renowned primatologist and anthropologist, Jane Goodall advocates for animal welfare and sustainable ecosystems.

Greta Thunberg

A climate activist from Sweden, Greta inspires young people globally to demand action against climate change.

Christiana Figueres

As a former UN Climate Chief, Christiana Figueres played a crucial role in the Paris Agreement.

Wangari Maathai

A Nobel Peace Prize winner, Wangari Maathai founded the Green Belt Movement, promoting reforestation and women's empowerment.

Vanessa Nakate

Ugandan climate activist Vanessa Nakate focuses on climate justice and the voices of those in the Global South.

Mary Robinson

As the first female president of Ireland, Mary Robinson advocates for climate justice and human rights.

Women in Climate Leadership Statistics

  • Women represent 55% of the global climate workforce.
  • Over 60% of community-based climate initiatives are led by women.
  • Women are more likely to implement sustainable practices in agriculture.
  • Female leaders are associated with higher climate ambition.
